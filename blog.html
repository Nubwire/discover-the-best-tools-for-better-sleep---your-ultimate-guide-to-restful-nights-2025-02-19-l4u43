The Science of Sleep: Understanding Sleep Cycles and Circadian Rhythms

Discover the intricate science behind sleep cycles and circadian rhythms in this comprehensive guide. Sleep consists of multiple 90-120 minute cycles, alternating between Non-REM and REM stages, each serving crucial functions for physical and mental restoration. Your circadian rhythm, regulated by factors like light exposure and temperature, controls your 24-hour sleep-wake cycle. Modern challenges like screen time and irregular schedules can disrupt these natural patterns. Learn evidence-based strategies to optimize your sleep, including maintaining consistent schedules, controlling light exposure, and creating ideal sleep environments. Understanding these mechanisms is key to achieving better rest and overall well-being.
